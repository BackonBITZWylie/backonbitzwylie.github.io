While the main story of Five Nights at Freddy's is told through the games, several book series expand the universe and provide additional lore. These books exist in separate but parallel continuities, meaning their events are not exactly the same as the game timeline, but they often reveal background information about characters, technology, and concepts that also exist in the games. Scott Cawthon has stated that the books are meant to fill in blanks about the lore rather than directly retell the game story. Many ideas introduced in the books later appear in the games in altered forms.

Game Continuity

The primary book continuities include: The Novel Trilogy (The Silver Eyes, The Twisted Ones, The Fourth Closet), Fazbear Frights short story series, Tales from the Pizzaplex

Early 1980s

Henry Emily and William Afton operate Fredbear’s Family Diner, similar to the game timeline. Henry is a brilliant roboticist who specializes in lifelike animatronics capable of complex movements and behaviors. At some point during this time, William Afton murders Henry’s daughter Charlotte Emily outside the restaurant, much like in the game timeline.

However, the novel continuity diverges significantly afterward. Henry becomes emotionally devastated by the loss of his daughter. In an attempt to cope with the grief, he secretly begins constructing a series of highly advanced robotic replicas of Charlotte. These robots are designed to grow and age, each representing different stages of Charlie’s life. Unknown to most people, the Charlie seen in the novels is actually one of these robotic recreations, not the original child.

Mid-1980s

A tragedy known as the Missing Children Incident still occurs in this universe. William Afton murders several children inside Freddy Fazbear’s Pizza while wearing a Spring Bonnie costume. As in the games, the victims’ spirits become attached to the animatronics.

However, the haunted animatronics behave somewhat differently in the novels, appearing less as wandering machines and more as controlled manifestations of the children’s spirits. Freddy’s locations eventually close due to the disappearances and growing public fear. William Afton is arrested for the murders but is later released due to lack of physical evidence linking him to the crimes.

Approximately 10 Years Later

The events of The Silver Eyes take place. Charlie and several childhood friends return to their hometown years after the Freddy’s tragedies. They explore the abandoned Freddy Fazbear’s Pizza building and discover the decaying animatronics still inside.

During their investigation, they encounter William Afton, who has taken on the false identity of a security guard named Dave Miller. It is revealed that Afton has been secretly returning to the abandoned restaurant for years. Eventually the animatronics attack Afton, and he is forced to hide inside the Spring Bonnie suit. A springlock failure occurs, crushing him inside the suit and trapping him just like in the games. However, in the novel continuity Afton becomes trapped in a different animatronic form known as Spring Bonnie / Springtrap much earlier in the timeline.

Shortly After

The events of The Twisted Ones occur. New animatronics known as the Twisted Animatronics begin appearing. Unlike the normal Freddy characters, these machines are extremely aggressive and appear monstrous when active. They are designed with special illusion technology that causes them to appear different depending on who is looking at them. This illusion technology works by projecting sound frequencies that alter how the human brain interprets what it sees.

The Twisted animatronics were created by William Afton before his apparent death and hidden underground near the old Freddy’s locations. The illusion technology introduced in this book later becomes important to fan theories explaining hallucinations seen in the games, such as Phantom animatronics or Nightmare animatronics.

Final Events of the Trilogy

The final novel, The Fourth Closet, reveals the full truth behind Charlie’s identity. Charlie discovers that she is not the original Charlotte Emily but rather one of several robotic copies built by Henry. Each robot contains fragments of memories meant to simulate real human development. The real Charlotte died years earlier.

The novel also reveals that William Afton has continued experimenting with remnant, the supernatural substance created when haunted metal is melted and infused with emotional energy. Afton injects remnant into machines in an attempt to achieve immortality. By the end of the story, Afton is defeated and destroyed, and many of the haunted spirits are finally released.

Fazbear Frights Series

The Fazbear Frights books are a large anthology of short horror stories connected to the FNaF universe. While many stories are self-contained, some connect together through an overarching narrative involving several key characters. The most important characters in this narrative include: Andrew, Jake, William Afton (as a burned corpse).

Andrew and the Vengeful Spirit

One of the most important revelations from Fazbear Frights involves the character Andrew. Andrew is a child whose spirit attaches itself to William Afton after his death. Rather than allowing Afton to die, Andrew intentionally keeps him alive in a constant state of suffering.

This idea closely parallels the events of Ultimate Custom Night, where a mysterious vengeful spirit traps Afton in an endless nightmare. Many fans believe Andrew represents the book version of the vengeful spirit, similar to Cassidy in the games.

The Stitchwraith

Another major character is the Stitchwraith, a mysterious animatronic made from parts of different machines. The Stitchwraith is eventually revealed to contain two spirits: Jake and Andrew. Jake is a peaceful child who wants to help others, while Andrew is consumed by anger toward Afton.

The Stitchwraith spends much of the series collecting haunted objects connected to Fazbear Entertainment. These objects contain fragments of emotional energy and remnant. This storyline introduces the idea that haunted energy can attach itself to objects, not just animatronics.

William Afton's Survival

Throughout the Fazbear Frights epilogue storyline, William Afton exists as a burned and partially destroyed body connected to remnant. Despite being severely damaged, Afton refuses to die due to the supernatural energy keeping him alive.

Eventually Afton attempts to rebuild himself using pieces of machinery and remnant collected from haunted objects. This concept is extremely similar to later events in the game timeline involving Burntrap and Glitchtrap, reinforcing the idea that Afton repeatedly attempts to return after being destroyed.

Tales from the Pizzaplex

The Tales from the Pizzaplex series is the book collection most closely connected to the modern FNaF games. Many of the locations and technologies described in these stories directly appear in Security Breach. The books also expand the backstory of the Mimic and Fazbear Entertainment’s modern experiments.

The Mimic Program

Several stories reveal the history of the Mimic AI. Originally designed by engineer Edwin Murray, the Mimic was meant to observe human behavior and replicate it to entertain children. However, after witnessing violent actions and emotional trauma, the Mimic begins copying aggressive behavior.

Fazbear Entertainment later duplicates the Mimic program and installs versions of it into multiple systems. This creates a network of Mimic-based AI controlling animatronics, software systems, and virtual environments.

The Construction of the Mega Pizzaplex

The books also reveal that the Freddy Fazbear Mega Pizzaplex was built on top of the ruins of older Freddy Fazbear locations. This includes underground structures connected to earlier restaurants and possibly the remains of Henry’s final fire.

While constructing the Pizzaplex, Fazbear Entertainment workers encounter strange animatronic machines hidden beneath the building. One of these machines is implied to be the original Mimic endoskeleton. The company seals the Mimic underground after several violent incidents involving construction workers.

The Modern Fazbear Corporation

Tales from the Pizzaplex also reveals that the modern version of Fazbear Entertainment is a massive corporate entity focused on entertainment technology, robotics, and virtual experiences. However, the company repeatedly ignores safety warnings and continues using dangerous experimental technologies.

This eventually leads to the creation of systems such as: The V.A.N.N.I. mask network, The Mimic1 digital program, Advanced animatronic AI used in the Pizzaplex. These systems later become central to the events of Help Wanted, Security Breach, and Ruin.
