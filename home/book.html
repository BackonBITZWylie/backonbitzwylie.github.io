The Five Night at Freddy's (FNAF) timeline is complex, spanninng decades across games and books that exist in seperate, though parallel, continuities. The following details the novel/book continuity using evidence from the sources.

Game Continuity

The books exist in separate, self-contained continuities but share lore elements and themes.

Background

Silver Eyes/Twisted Ones/Fourth Closet

This trilogy follows Charlie, Henry Emily's daughter who did not die as a child in this timeline. The books explore a different version of the MCI and Afton's crimes in the town of Hurricane, Utah. Unlike the games, this continuity presents alternate versions of events and characters, offering insight into motivations and scientific concepts behind possession.

1985

MCI

The MCI happens in Hurricane, Utah. William Afton, operating under the alias “Dave Miller,” murders multiple children at Freddy Fazbear’s Pizza. Their spirits possess the animatronics, similar to the game continuity.

1995

Silver Eyes

The main events of The Silver Eyes take place, following Charlie and her friends revisiting the abandoned Freddy Fazbear's Pizza ten years later. William Afton is revealed to still be alive and hiding in plain sight. The book introduces the idea that Afton studies and manipulates possession.

Later

Twisted Ones

The Twisted Ones introduces the “Twisted” animatronics, advanced illusion-based machines designed by Afton. These animatronics can alter their appearance using sound illusion discs. The novel expands on the idea that Afton intentionally experiments with fear and suffering.

Later

Fourth Closet

The Fourth Closet reveals that Charlie herself is an advanced robotic recreation built by Henry after the death of his real daughter. The book heavily explores remnant, describing it as a substance that can bind souls to metal. The story concludes with Afton’s defeat and destruction, though this ending is specific to the novel continuity.

Background

Fazbear Frights

Fazbear Frights is an anthology series consisting of short horror stories that parallel events and themes from the games. While many stories are standalone, several introduce concepts that appear to connect directly to the game continuity.

Various Dates

The stories span multiple decades. “Into the Pit” takes place in 1979 and features a time-traveling ball pit connected to an MCI-like event. “The Man in Room 1280” depicts a severely burned man kept alive by a vengeful spirit, widely interpreted as a parallel to William Afton after FNaF 6 and connected to Ultimate Custom Night. Other stories introduce the concept of agony, an emotional energy that can animate objects similarly to remnant.

Various Dates

Evidence Connection

Fazbear Frights is widely believed to clarify ambiguous game lore. The concept of agony helps explain how non-possessed objects can become hostile. The Stitchwraith epilogues connect multiple stories together and strongly parallel Golden Freddy, Cassidy, and post-fire Afton interpretations.

Background

Tales from the Pizzaplex

Tales from the Pizzaplex is another anthology series that is much more directly tied to the Security Breach era of the games. Many characters, locations, and technologies from these books appear explicitly in the game continuity.

1970s-1980s (Backstory)

Tales from the Pizzaplex

The Mimic endoskeleton and Mimic1 program are created by Edwin Murray to mimic human behavior. After witnessing trauma and violence, the Mimic begins copying aggressive actions. Fazbear Entertainment later acquires and repurposes the Mimic technology.

Modern Era

Tales from the Pizzaplex

Several stories take place within or around the Mega Pizzaplex. These include malfunctioning animatronics, virtual reality simulations, and AI-based threats tied to the Mimic1 system.

Background

The Security Breach Files

This in-universe guidebook compiles blueprints, hidden messages, and environmental storytelling details from Security Breach and its DLC. While partially a strategy guide, it also provides clarification on certain story elements and reinforces the connection between the games and Tales from the Pizzaplex.

Background

Interactive Novels

More recent interactive FNaF novels allow readers to make choices that affect the story’s outcome. These books often present alternate scenarios but still incorporate recurring concepts such as remnant, AI mimicry, and Fazbear Entertainment’s corporate cover-ups. While their exact canon status varies, they continue expanding the broader FNaF universe.
