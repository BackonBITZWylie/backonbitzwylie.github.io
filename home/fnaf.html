The Five Nights at Freddy's (FNaF) timeline is complex, spanning decades across games and books that exist in separate, though parallel, continuities. The following details the game continuity (including in-game books, hidden minigames, and files) and the novel continuity, using evidence from the sources. Many events in the timeline are confirmed through gameplay and developer statements from Scott Cawthon, while others are inferred through hidden minigames, environmental storytelling, Fazbear Entertainment files, and connections to the books. Because the franchise intentionally leaves some mysteries unresolved, several parts of the timeline are based on widely accepted fan interpretations supported by in-game evidence.

Game Continuity Timeline (Core Lore) The main timeline of events depicted or referenced in the games and in-game books is as follows:

1979

Edwin Murray, a robotics engineer connected to Fazbear Entertainment, creates the Mimic endoskeleton and the Mimic1 AI program. The Mimic is designed to observe human behavior and replicate it for use in entertainment animatronics.

After Edwin experiences a traumatic loss involving his son David, he violently destroys machines in front of the Mimic. The Mimic learns aggression and destructive behavior from observing Edwin. This moment is critical because the Mimic’s programming revolves around copying human actions without understanding morality, which eventually allows it to mimic William Afton’s violent tendencies.

The Mimic technology is later acquired and repurposed by Fazbear Entertainment and becomes the foundation for several later AI systems used in animatronics and virtual experiences. Some theories suggest the Mimic program may have influenced early Fazbear animatronics long before the events of the modern games.

Late 1970s-Early 1980s

Fredbear's Family Diner is founded by William Afton and Henry Emily, who form Fazbear Entertainment together. The diner features the first springlock animatronics, Fredbear and Spring Bonnie, which can function both as wearable mascot suits and robotic performers. These suits use dangerous springlock mechanisms that hold back internal mechanical parts while worn by a person.

A very early stage show known as Fredbear’s Singing Show may have existed prior to the diner, suggesting that Fazbear Entertainment experimented with animatronic entertainment even before opening their first restaurant. This concept is referenced in Security Breach collectible items and marketing materials. During this period, Henry designs the Puppet (Security Puppet) as a protective animatronic meant to monitor children and ensure they remain safe inside the restaurant.

One night during a rainy evening, Henry's daughter Charlotte Emily (Charlie) is locked outside the building by bullies and murdered by William Afton in an alley. The Puppet escapes the building but arrives too late to save her. Charlotte’s soul possesses the Puppet, making it the first known haunted animatronic in the timeline. This event is shown in the “Take Cake to the Children” and Security Puppet minigames. Charlotte’s possession of the Puppet becomes extremely important later because the Puppet appears capable of granting life to other animatronics, essentially acting as the force that binds souls to machines.

1983

Events of Five Nights at Freddy's 4 minigames take place. William Afton’s younger son, commonly referred to by fans as the Crying Child or Evan, is terrified of the animatronics and repeatedly bullied by his older brother Michael Afton and his friends.

During his birthday party at Fredbear’s Family Diner, Michael and the other bullies place him inside Fredbear’s mouth as a prank. The animatronic’s springlock jaw closes due to mechanical failure, causing the Bite of ’83. The Crying Child dies later in the hospital.

The in-game TV displays a “Fredbear and Friends” cartoon with the date 1983, confirming the year and showing that Freddy Fazbear, Bonnie, Chica, and Foxy were already established characters by this time.

Several mysteries originate here:

1. The identity of the Crying Child’s spirit

2. The meaning of the phrase “I will put you back together”

3. The origin of Golden Freddy

Some theories suggest the Crying Child’s spirit may later connect to Golden Freddy or be assisted by the Puppet in the “Happiest Day” event. Another theory known as Shattered Souls suggests his spirit may be split across multiple animatronics.

Mid-1980's (Likely 1985)

The Missing Children Incident (MCI) occurs at a Freddy Fazbear’s Pizza location. William Afton, wearing the Spring Bonnie suit, lures at least five children into a hidden safe room and murders them.

These children are widely believed to be: Gabriel, Jeremy, Susie, Fritz, Cassidy. The Puppet gives life to the animatronics, allowing the children’s spirits to possess: Freddy Fazbear, Bonnie, Chica, Foxy, Golden Freddy

Golden Freddy becomes one of the most mysterious animatronics in the series. Many theorists believe Golden Freddy may contain two spirits: Cassidy and the Crying Child, known as the GoldenDuo theory. Golden Freddy demonstrates supernatural abilities not seen in other animatronics, including teleportation, hallucination creation, and reality distortion.

Mid-1980's (Shortly after the MCI)

William Afton creates Circus Baby’s Pizza World through Afton Robotics. Unlike previous animatronics, these machines are secretly designed with child-capturing mechanisms hidden inside their bodies. Examples include: Internal storage tanks, Claw capture devices, Voice mimicry systems.

William’s daughter Elizabeth Afton approaches Circus Baby despite being warned to stay away. Baby’s internal claw captures and kills her. The restaurant immediately shuts down, officially blamed on a gas leak.

The animatronics are transferred to Circus Baby’s Entertainment and Rental, an underground facility where they are rented out for private parties and secretly used for experiments involving remnant and possession. Remnant is a supernatural substance created when a haunted metal object is melted and infused with emotional energy. William becomes obsessed with using remnant to achieve immortality.

1987

The gameplay of Five Nights at Freddy's 2 occurs at a newer Freddy Fazbear’s Pizza location. New Toy Animatronics are introduced with: facial recognition software, criminal database connections, mobility during the daytime.

Hidden minigames suggest another series of murders occurs during this time, often called the “Save Them” incident. The Puppet again appears to give life to the victims. The location closes after the Bite of ’87, when an animatronic bites a person’s frontal lobe during a daytime event. The victim survives but loses their frontal lobe. Because of this incident, animatronics are no longer allowed to roam freely during the day. The Toy animatronics are scrapped while the older models are kept for possible future use.

Early-to-mid 1990s (c.1993)

The gameplay of Five Nights at Freddy's takes place. The animatronics are now the older classic models possessed by the original missing children. The restaurant struggles financially and eventually closes.

The security guard Mike Schmidt is widely believed to be Michael Afton, working under an alias while searching for his father. Phone Guy recordings mention that the animatronics behave aggressively toward adults but seem harmless around children. This may indicate they recognize Michael as someone connected to William Afton.

Late 1990s (Post-FNAF 1 Closure)

William Afton returns to the abandoned Freddy’s location and dismantles the animatronics, likely attempting to extract remnant. The spirits of the murdered children appear and corner him.

In desperation, he hides inside the Spring Bonnie suit. Moisture triggers a springlock failure, crushing him inside the suit and trapping him as Springtrap. The room is sealed afterward, hiding his body for decades.

Unspecified Time (Post-1993, Pre-2023)

The events of Five Nights at Freddy's: Sister Location take place at Circus Baby’s Entertainment and Rental. Michael Afton is sent there, possibly by William, with instructions to “put her back together.” Over several nights he interacts with the Funtime animatronics.

Eventually the animatronics merge into a single entity called Ennard. Michael is scooped by the Scooper, allowing Ennard to wear his body as a disguise and escape to the surface. After his body decays, Ennard abandons him. However, Michael survives due to the remnant injected into his body.

A major fan theory known as MoltenMCI suggests that William Afton melted down the original animatronics to extract remnant and injected it into the Funtime animatronics. This would mean the souls of the original missing children eventually become part of Molten Freddy. Many fans consider this theory strongly supported by dialogue in Pizzeria Simulator.

2023

The gameplay of Five Nights at Freddy's 3 takes place thirty years after the final Freddy’s location closed. A horror attraction called Fazbear’s Fright is created using salvaged items from previous locations. Springtrap is discovered inside a sealed safe room and becomes the main attraction. The building eventually burns down. However, Springtrap survives the fire.

Shortly After (2023)

The events of Freddy Fazbear's Pizzeria Simulator occur. Henry Emily creates a new restaurant as a trap designed to gather the remaining haunted animatronics. The animatronics that appear include: Scraptrap (William Afton), Scrap Baby (Elizabeth Afton), Molten Freddy, Lefty (containing the Puppet).

Henry then sets the building on fire. This fire is intended to free the trapped souls and end the cycle permanently. Michael Afton dies in the fire along with the animatronics.

Post-2023

Ultimate Custom Night represents William Afton’s punishment. A vengeful spirit, believed to be Cassidy, traps him in an endless nightmare where animatronics repeatedly attack him. Some theories suggest this is not literal hell, but rather a mental prison created by Golden Freddy.

Late 2020s (c. 2025-2029)

Five Nights at Freddy's: Help Wanted is created as a VR game meant to repair the public image of Fazbear Entertainment. The company claims the previous tragedies were exaggerated rumors. However, old circuit boards are scanned to recreate animatonic behavior.

This accidentally recreates a digital entity known as Glitchtrap. Originally believed to be William Afton’s digital ghost, later lore strongly suggests Glitchtrap is actually the Mimic1 AI copying Afton’s personality and behavior. Glitchtrap begins manipulating the player and eventually brainwashes a tester named Vanessa.

Late 2020s (c. 2025-2029)

Fazbear Entertainment launches a service delivering animatronics directly to customers’ homes. Emails in the game reveal that: Vanessa works as a security guard for the company, A strange virus is spreading through the animatronic network, The Mimic program may be controlling the systems.

Early 2030's (c. 2029-2031)

Fazbear Entertainment opens the massive Freddy Fazbear Mega Pizzaplex. The complex includes shopping malls, arcades, and themed attractions. Gregory, a mysterious child trapped overnight in the building, teams up with Glamrock Freddy to survive the night. The animatronics behave aggressively due to corruption in the system.

Gregory eventually discovers underground ruins of an older Freddy’s location beneath the Pizzaplex. Deep underground he encounters Burntrap, a damaged animatronic body apparently controlled by either William Afton or the Mimic copying him. Many newer theories believe Burntrap was never actually William, but rather the Mimic using Afton’s identity.

Early 2030s (c. 2029-2031)

Five Nights at Freddy's: Help Wanted 2. This game reveals more about Fazbear Entertainment’s internal systems and the V.A.N.N.I. mask, which allows users to see digital layers of reality connected to the Mimic network.

The mask appears to connect the real world with a hidden digital system used by Fazbear Entertainment. Vanessa’s role and identity are explored further, including hints about Vanny, the masked follower of Glitchtrap.

Early 2030's (C. 2029-2032)

The ruined Pizzaplex is explored by a new character named Cassie. Cassie receives guidance from a voice pretending to be Gregory. It is eventually revealed that this voice is actually the Mimic, which has learned to perfectly copy Gregory’s voice.

Deep beneath the Pizzaplex, Cassie encounters the Mimic animatronic, a skeletal machine capable of altering its shape and behavior to mimic others. The Mimic is revealed to have been trapped underground long before the Pizzaplex was built, suggesting Fazbear Entertainment sealed it away after earlier incidents. The DLC strongly implies that the Mimic—not William Afton—is now the central antagonist of the modern FNaF storyline.
